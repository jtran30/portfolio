The Problem with AR

The problem with AR nowadays involves with capability and consistency of the augmentation in AR. Such problems include the AR app being limited to very specific situations or scenarios such as the user has to stand in one spot (or a specific spot) in order to use the app. If the user doesn't have to stand in one spot, then using the AR otherwise would normally be very dangerous. For example, AR in driving, or even walking in general could pose a threat to the user, even though if the AR application works such as a AR GPS which some apps are trying to achieve.

If it isn't involving location or orientation of the user, then another problem in AR would be its usefulness in a variety of situations. For example, some current developments in AR is like what we mentioned, the AR GPS. The fundamental problem in this is: what benefit does this app provide over using a traditional GPS system? It could be distracting to user, and it's not entirely accurate (at the moment).

Onto the consistency of the AR apps, multiple AR apps cease to function properly if the user was to move the camera a lot or have shaky hands. The AR wouldn't generated in the proper position or may appear to be very jittery. There are some attempts to fix currently, which is to either change algorithm of the app (which is still in development), or to improve (or purposely) the hardware such as the camera to support these said issues.



Our solutions

Our proposal to some of these issues in AR is as follows.

Simplify AR

AR needs to be simplified to an extent so that AR could effectively and safely do its job (without distracting the user). So for example, the AR GPS could be integrated into a windshield of a car, so that user does not need to take their eyes off the road. This may seem dangerous at first, but if we minimized the items we place onto the windshield, it should be safe and quicker for the user to process this information. These items can include simple things (no 3d rendered objects to be augmented in reality), such as the next name, direction arrow, and perhaps current street location.


Implement AR

Currently AR's sole purpose is to augment the reality through the camera/hardware, but there isn't many things to help supplement the AR itself. We introduce that some things could assist AR in everyday situations. Such as sensors in street poles or signs, other phones, or building entrances, which sends out signals so that the phone can receive information on the signal. The information could be the location, address, or some other data about the item. So how this would help AR is through assisting the AR's app know more about what it's currently seeing, and could provide more useful and accurate information to the user.
